About Me

I am an interdisciplinary computational scientist, I focus on designing scalable tensor engines, which serve as the mathematical foundation for both next-generation quantum simulations and modern AI/Large Language Model (LLM) architectures. Currently a postdoctoral researcher at a CNRS quantum chemistry laboratory in Toulouse, I am developing a tensor toolchain for an Advanced ERC grant. I work on automatic optimization of tensor expressions and enabling distributed memory tensor contractions on GPUs with excellent weak scaling. Recently, I was awarded the Marie Curie Fellowship Seal of Excellence (92% score) for architecting a novel HPC tensor library utilizing theory of representations and graph theory.
