About Me

I am an interdisciplinary computational scientist with over eight years of experience in architecting high-performance computing (HPC) solutions. My core expertise lies in designing scalable, distributed tensor engines for GPUs, which are fundamental to both modern AI/Large Language Models and next-generation quantum simulations.

Currently a postdoctoral researcher at CNRS in Toulouse, I am developing a novel tensor toolchain for an Advanced ERC grant. My work focuses on the automatic optimization of tensor expressions and enabling distributed memory tensor contractions on GPUs with excellent weak scaling. I was recently awarded the Marie Curie Fellowship Seal of Excellence with a 91% score for my work on a novel HPC tensor library optimized for Grace-Hopper architecture.
